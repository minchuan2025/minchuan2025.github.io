Improving Flow Matching based Text-to-Speech with Preference aligned Optimization and Classifier-Free Guidance

Minchuan Chen, Chenchen Wan, Peng Qi, Shaojun Wang, Jing Xiao

Ping An Technology, China Shanghai Jiao Tong University, China Shanghai Jiao Tong University Chongqing Artificial Intelligence Research Institute, China

Abstract

Flow matching is a powerful generative model and has become a core component of modern text-to-speech (TTS) systems. In order to ensure high-quality speech synthesis, Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG) is widely used during training and inference stages of flow-matching-based TTS models. However, in the scenario of zero-shot voice cloning, the models keep human feedback isolated from training and inference, resulting in mismatched training objectives and evaluation metrics. In this work, we propose a preference alignment method by separately modeling preferred and dispreferred information with two distinct models, which enables better utilization of preferred and dispreferred data. We further incorporate novel classifier-free guidance techniques to further improve synthesis adherence to the text and reference audio. The experiments demonstrate that these optimizations significantly enhance intelligibility, target speaker similarity and naturalness of synthesized speech. We also verify that the proposed model outperforms the baseline TTS model, despite being trained on a significantly smaller dataset.
